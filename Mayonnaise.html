Mayonnaise PH

Mayonnaise, also known as "Mayo," is a Filipino alternative rock band formed in 2002,
consisting of Monty Macalino on vocals and guitar (the band's founder and primary songwriter),
Paga Manikan on lead guitar, Carlo Servano on bass, and Shan Regalado on drums.
Their music, characterized by a blend of alternative rock, pop-rock, and a touch of emo,
draws inspiration from various genres and artists, contributing to their unique sound.
The band's self-titled debut album, released in 2004, featured the hit single "Jopay," which remains one of their most popular songs.

Band History

Mayonnaise is a well-known rock band from the Philippines, formed in 2002. The band is recognized for its catchy melodies,
heartfelt lyrics, and a blend of alternative rock and pop rock influences. Here are some key points about the band:

1. Formation and Name: The band was formed in 2002 and is named after the song "Mayonnaise" by the Smashing Pumpkins.

2. Members: The original lineup included Monty Macalino (vocals and guitar), Paga Manikan (bass), Lee Maningas (drums), and Shan Regalado (drums).
Over the years, the lineup has seen changes, with Monty Macalino remaining the consistent member and frontman.

3. Breakthrough: Mayonnaise gained prominence after winning the "Red Horse Muziklaban" rock band competition in 2004. This victory helped them gain a significant following in the local music scene.
4. Debut Album: Their self-titled debut album, released in 2004, included the hit single "Jopay," which remains one of their most popular songs to date.
